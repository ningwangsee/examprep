# Chapter 3c: Special Circumstances and Emergencies
Source: https://www.pa.gov/agencies/dmv/driver-services/pennsylvania-drivers-manual/online-drivers-manual/special-circumstances-and-emergencies

## Work Zones
- Turn on headlights (not just daytime running lights) in active work zones
- Double your following distance
- Do not cross solid white lines
- Do not use cruise control
- Obey flaggers — their authority overrides traffic signals
- Fines are DOUBLED for violations in active work zones
- Certain violations result in license suspension
- ~40,000 people injured or killed annually in U.S. work zone crashes

## Railroad Crossings
- Every 1.5 hours, a train collides with a vehicle or pedestrian in U.S.
- 40x more likely to die in train crash than vehicle crash
- Most crossings have only warning signs and crossbucks; many lack gates
- Look left, right, then left again at all crossings
- Never pass another vehicle at or approaching a crossing
- Don't follow another vehicle blindly; ensure you have room to cross
- If trapped on tracks with approaching train: EXIT vehicle immediately and move FAR from tracks in the direction the train came from

## Sharing Road: Pedestrians
- Yield at intersections and crosswalks
- Right turn on red: stop, look all directions, yield to pedestrians
- Reduce speed around children; they may run into traffic
- Extra caution with elderly and disabled pedestrians
- Check for pedestrians before backing up
- Yield to blind pedestrians (white cane, guide dog, being led)
- Never wave pedestrians into traffic at unmarked/mid-block locations

## Sharing Road: Motorcyclists and Moped Drivers
- Allow full lane use
- Most motorcycle crashes at intersections (vehicle turning left in front)
- Don't assume signal means turning; signals may not auto-cancel
- Allow for sudden lane/speed changes to avoid road hazards
- Maintain 4-second following distance minimum; increase in poor conditions
- Obstacles minor to cars can be deadly to riders

## Sharing Road: Trucks and Buses
### Passing a Truck
- At highway speeds, can take up to 30 seconds to safely pass a truck
- Only move back after seeing truck's headlights/front grill in rearview mirror
- Uphill: easier to pass (truck loses speed); Downhill: harder (truck gains speed)
- Keep both hands on wheel for turbulence effects; don't brake/stop suddenly after passing

### Following a Truck
- At least 4 seconds needed to stay out of truck's rear blind spot
- Trucks need longer stopping distances
- Loaded truck at 65 mph: minimum 600 feet to stop (passenger car: ~400 feet)
- Leave room on hills; trucks may roll backward

### Oversized Loads
- Accompanied by pilot vehicles; may cross yellow/white lines due to excessive width

### Escape Ramps
- For trucks out of control or with brake failure ONLY
- Never stop/park near escape ramps

## Emergency Vehicles (Fire, Police, Ambulance, Blood Delivery, Rescue)
- Pull to curb/side, drive parallel to curb, STOP
- One-way street: drive toward nearest roadside and stop
- Stay clear of intersections
- After passing: maintain at least 500 feet distance
- Ensure no other emergency vehicle approaching

## Steer Clear Law (effective September 8, 2006)
- Move into non-adjacent lane past emergency response area
- Emergency response includes: police, sheriffs, coroners, medical examiners, firefighters, fire police, fire marshals, rescue personnel, EMS, towing/recovery, hazmat, highway construction/maintenance
- If unable to move over: reduce speed
- Penalties (summary offense):
  - 1st offense: up to $250
  - 2nd offense: $500
  - 3rd+: $1,000
  - Work zone violations: doubled
  - Serious injury/death: 90-day suspension + up to $1,000 fine
  - Highway worker/emergency responder killed/injured: up to 1-year suspension + up to $10,000 fine

## Passengers in Open-Bed Trucks
- Max speed: 35 mph if any person in truck bed
- No persons under 18 in open truck bed
- Exceptions: farmer's child, farm labor child, hunting license holder during season, sanctioned parade

## Snow Plows
- Limited visibility in mirrors; drivers can't see behind
- Wing plows extend several feet on either side
- Don't attempt to pass on left or right

## Road Rage
- ~60% of ~1,500 PA annual highway deaths attributed to aggressive driving
- Never retaliate; don't try to teach a lesson
- If pursued: drive to police station, convenience store, or public location with witnesses

## Driving Emergencies
- 37% of crash drivers took no action to avoid the crash
- Above 25 mph: steering usually better than braking (faster reaction to swerve than brake)

### Steering Technique
- 9 & 3 or 8 & 4 o'clock hand positions (thumbs along wheel face, NOT inside rim)
- Keep at least 10 inches from airbag

### ABS vs. Non-ABS Braking
- Without ABS: pump brakes (press and release repeatedly); slamming locks wheels
- With ABS: maintain firm, continuous pressure; do NOT pump pedal; don't be alarmed by noises/pulsations

### Skidding
- Look and steer where you WANT to go
- Rear skids left → turn wheel left; rear skids right → turn wheel right
- DO NOT use brakes during skid; wheels lock and steering is lost
- Second skid may occur in opposite direction but less severe

### Flooded Roadways
- 6 inches: can cause loss of control/float small vehicles
- 2 feet: carries away most vehicles including SUVs
- Illegal to drive around/through signs closing road due to hazardous conditions

### Stuck Accelerator
1. Tap pedal or lift with toe (don't reach with hand)
2. If stuck: shift to NEUTRAL; use brakes (engine will race; power removed from wheels)
3. Steer off road; stop; turn off engine; turn on emergency flashers
- Emergency: Turn ignition to OFF (NOT LOCK — locks steering); apply brakes

### Brake Failure (Older Vehicles)
1. Shift to low gear; look for stopping place
2. Pump brake pedal quickly several times (builds pressure)
3. Use parking/emergency brake if pumping fails; keep release button ready
4. Last resort: turn ignition OFF (not LOCK); shift to lowest gear (may damage transmission)
- Never drive after brake failure; call for help

### Tire Blowout
1. Hold steering wheel tightly
2. STAY OFF BRAKES (causes skidding/loss of control)
3. Slowly remove foot from gas
4. Steer smoothly; no large/jerky actions
5. Ride flat tire to safe pullover spot
- Front blowout: steering wheel vibrates, vehicle pulls to side
- Rear blowout: corner drops suddenly, rear wobbles

### Vehicle Drifts Off Pavement
1. Ease off gas (don't brake suddenly); gradual braking only
2. Steer parallel to roadway, straddling edge
3. Slow gradually to under 25 mph (preferably ~10 mph)
4. Check rear traffic; signal left
5. Gently steer left to ease wheels back on pavement; then gently steer right to straighten

### Head-On Collision Avoidance
1. Honk horn to attract attention
2. Try to escape RIGHT (never swerve left)
3. If unavoidable: brake firmly and steadily; every mph slower reduces impact

## Funeral Procession
- Yield to procession; lead vehicle must complete full stop at stop sign
- May proceed through red light if lead vehicle started through on green
- Must yield to emergency vehicles
- May display flashing/revolving purple light
- Don't cut in/out unless directed by police or funeral director
