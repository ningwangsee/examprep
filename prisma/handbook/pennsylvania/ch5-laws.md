# Chapter 5: Laws and Related Issues
Source: https://www.pa.gov/agencies/dmv/driver-services/pennsylvania-drivers-manual/online-drivers-manual/chapter-5-laws-and-related-issues

## Name / Address Changes
- Must notify PennDOT within 15 days of name or address change
- Online or by calling 717-412-5300
- Name change: complete Form DL-80
- Driver's license update cards issued at no cost to non-commercial drivers

## Seat Belt Law
- All drivers and front-seat passengers must wear seat belts
- Passengers ages 8–17 must wear seat belts regardless of seating position
- Drivers under 18 cannot exceed number of available seat belts
- At 30 mph, impact = falling from 3-story building; fatally injured at speeds as low as 12 mph

## Child Restraint Law (Passenger Protection Act 229)
- Under 4: rear-facing child safety seat (rear-facing until outgrowing manufacturer limits)
- Ages 4–7: booster seat with lap and shoulder belts
- Ages 8–17: seat belt, any seating position
- NEVER place rear-facing seat in front seat with active airbag
- Violations: fine + court costs; drivers are responsible

## Vehicle Heatstroke Prevention
- Interior temperatures reach dangerous levels within 10 minutes when outside temp is in low 80s°F, even with windows partially open
- Never leave children or pets unattended in vehicles

## Airbag Safety
- Maintain at least 10 inches distance from steering wheel
- Hold wheel at 9 & 3 or 8 & 4 o'clock
- Children 12 and under: ride in rear seats
- Never place infant in rear-facing seat in front seat with passenger-side airbag

## ADA Services
- All Driver License Centers and Photo License Centers physically accessible
- Free interpreters for deaf/hearing-impaired: request at least 2 weeks in advance
- Contact: 717-412-5300 or TDD: 711
- PA Relay Service: 1-800-654-5984 (TDD) or 1-800-654-5988 (hearing)

## Parking for Persons With Disabilities
- Must display disabled person or disabled veteran registration plate/placard AND vehicle operated by/transporting the disabled person
- Violations: summary offense, fines $50–$200; vehicles may be towed

## Anti-Littering Law
- Dropping, throwing, depositing waste from vehicle prohibited
- Fine: up to $300 upon conviction
- Drivers responsible for violations by passengers

## Vehicle Safety Inspection
- Safety inspection: every 12 months
- May be inspected up to 90 days before expiration
- Emissions inspection required in many counties for 1975+ vehicles
- Emissions Customer Hotline: 1-800-265-0921

## Safe Passing and Bicycles
- Minimum 4 feet clearance when passing bicycles
- May cross yellow center lines to maintain clearance if safe
- Cannot force bicyclists off road (criminal charges possible)
- Bicyclists must obey traffic laws but may travel below minimum speed
- Bicyclists should maintain at least 3 feet distance from parked vehicles
- Dutch Reach: use far hand to open door; check mirrors for approaching cyclists

## Anti-Texting Law
- Effective: March 8, 2012
- Primary summary offense (police can stop you solely for this)
- Fine: $50
- Prohibited: using wireless phones, PDAs, smartphones, portable computers for texting, messaging, emailing, or browsing while driving
- Exceptions: GPS devices, vehicle-integrated systems, mass transit/school bus communication devices

## Obedience to Traffic-Control Devices Warning of Hazardous Conditions
- Effective: September 6, 2012
- Illegal to drive around/through signs closing roads due to hazardous conditions (flooding, etc.)
- Violations:
  - 2 points added to driving record
  - Fine: up to $250
  - If emergency responders called: up to $500 + full liability for emergency response costs
