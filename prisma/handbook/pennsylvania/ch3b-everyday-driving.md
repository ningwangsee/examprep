# Chapter 3b: Everyday Driving Skills
Source: https://www.pa.gov/agencies/dmv/driver-services/pennsylvania-drivers-manual/online-drivers-manual/everyday-driving-skills

## Managing Space (Following Distance)
- 4-second following distance rule on dry pavement
- How to measure: when rear bumper of vehicle ahead passes a landmark, count seconds until you reach same spot; less than 4 seconds = too close
- Increase distance when:
  - Wet, slippery, or unpaved roads
  - Following trucks, buses, vans, or trailers (blocked view)
  - Following school buses, taxis, hazmat trucks
  - Driving downhill (brakes less effective)
  - Stopped on uphill (vehicle ahead may roll backward)
- Lateral space: at least 8 feet of escape space on one side
- Maintain space from parked vehicles (door opening hazard)

## Managing Speed

### Speed Limits
- Maximum state speed: 70 mph
- School zones: 15 mph (when school zone lights flash or during posted times)
- Nighttime dark roads: don't exceed 45 mph (low beams illuminate ~250 ft; stopping distance at 55 mph exceeds 250 ft)
- Wet roads: reduce 5–10 mph; increase following distance to 5–6 seconds
- Packed snow: decrease speed by half
- Ice: slow to crawl speed; leave ~10 times normal space

### Headlight Requirements (must be ON when):
- Unable to see 1,000 feet ahead
- Between sunset and sunrise
- Unfavorable atmospheric conditions (rain, snow, sleet, hail, fog, smoke, smog)
- Driving through work zones
- Wipers in continuous or intermittent use (effective January 28, 2007)
- Insufficient light where vehicles blend with background
- Fine: starts at $25, total approaches $100

### Headlight Use Rules
- Low beams within 500 feet of oncoming vehicles
- Low beams when following within 300 feet
- Flash high beams briefly if blinded by high-beam vehicle
- Look toward right side of road if blinded; maintain course
- Fog: use low beams (fog lights if equipped); high beams reflect back and increase glare
- If fog too dense: pull completely off road and wait

### Penalties for Falling Snow/Ice from Vehicle
- Fines: $200–$1,000 per offense if causes injury or death

### Hydroplaning
- Can occur at speeds as low as 35 mph with 1/10 inch of water
- If hydroplaning: slowly release gas, keep hands on wheel, don't slam brakes or turn suddenly, brake gently if necessary

### Black Ice
- Thin ice on clear roadway when temperatures drop
- Road looks wet but vehicles show no tire spray

## Turn Signals and Communication
- Signal at least 100 feet before turning (under 35 mph)
- Signal at least 300 feet before turning (35 mph or faster)
- Center turn lanes: signal 3–4 seconds before entering; enter completely
- Hand signals: Left turn = arm extended left; Right turn = arm bent upward; Stop = arm bent downward
- Tap brakes 3–4 times when stopping unexpectedly
- Use hazard flashers if driving below 40 mph on limited-access highway

## Turning
- Left turns: as close to centerline as possible; on multi-lane: start from left lane, turn into closest lane
- Right turns: as close to right edge as possible; start from right lane, turn into closest lane
- U-turns: only when oncoming drivers are at least 500 feet away and clear view exists
- U-turns prohibited on curves, at hill crests, where "NO U-TURN" signs posted
- Right turn on red: only after stopping completely and yielding to pedestrians/traffic; exceptions: left on red from one-way to one-way
- Do not enter intersection if vehicles block path, even with green light

## Passing
- Go ~10 mph faster than vehicle you're passing
- Two-lane road at 45 mph: requires 1/4 mile (1,320 feet) of clear roadway
- Prohibited within 100 feet of intersection, railroad crossing, bridge, elevated structure, or tunnel
- Prohibited on curves or hill crests with limited visibility
- Prohibited on shoulder of multi-lane highway
- Illegal to pass vehicle ahead in acceleration lane
- Return to lane only after seeing both headlights of passed vehicle in rearview mirror
- Passing on right allowed when: vehicle ahead turning/signaling left (stay on berm/shoulder), OR roadway with 2+ marked lanes each direction

## Negotiating Intersections / Right-of-Way
- Drivers turning LEFT must yield to oncoming vehicles going straight
- Roundabouts: entering driver yields to circulating traffic
- Simultaneous approach: driver from LEFT yields to driver from RIGHT
- Vehicles from alleys/private roads/driveways must yield to main road
- Four-way stop: first arrival proceeds first; simultaneous = left yields to right
- Safe crossing gaps at 30 mph street:
  - Two-way stop crossing: ~6-second gap in both directions
  - Right turn: ~8-second gap
  - Left turn: ~9-second gap from approaching right-side traffic
- "Law does not give anyone the right-of-way; it only says who must yield"
- Over 1/3 of crashes involving 16-year-old PA drivers: pulling out from stop sign or turning left

## Parking
- Park no more than 12 inches from curb; no curb = pull as far off roadway as possible
- On downhill with curb: turn wheels completely to the right
- On uphill with curb: turn wheels to the left (away from curb)
- No curb: always turn wheels right toward shoulder
- Set parking brake; shift to Park (auto) or Reverse/First (manual)
- Vehicle must be visible 500 feet in both directions if stopped on roadway

### Prohibited Parking Distances
- Within 50 feet of railroad crossing rails
- Within 15 feet of a fire hydrant
- Within 20 feet of a crosswalk at an intersection
- Within 30 feet of flashing signals, stop signs, yield signs, or traffic control devices
- Within 20 feet of fire station driveway entrance
- Also prohibited: on sidewalks, within intersections, on crosswalks, on bridges, on railroad tracks, between divided highway roadways, double parking, in front of driveways

## Negotiating Highways / Interchanges
- At 65 mph, vehicle travels nearly 100 feet per second
- Half of fatal highway crashes involve vehicles running off road due to speeding and/or fatigue
- 20% of fatal highway crashes are rear-end collisions (inattention + tailgating)
- If you miss exit: proceed to NEXT exit; never stop/back up/swerve across lanes
- Weaving lanes: entering driver must yield to exiting driver
- Change only ONE lane at a time; signal every time
