# Chapter 3a: Choosing Safety First
Source: https://www.pa.gov/agencies/dmv/driver-services/pennsylvania-drivers-manual/online-drivers-manual/choosing-safety-first

## Vehicle Checks Before Driving

### Tire Safety
- Check tread depth monthly or before long trips using the Penny Test:
  - Hold penny with Lincoln's body between thumb and forefinger
  - Insert Lincoln's head into tire groove at lowest tread point
  - If ANY part of Lincoln's head is covered by tread = safe tread depth
- Check tire pressure "cold" — before driving or at least 3 hours after driving
- Use PSI from owner's manual or driver's side door jamb
- Keep in vehicle: spare tire, lug wrench, jack, flashlight, flares, reflective triangles, wheel wedges, tire repair kit, gloves

### Fluids
- Check engine oil, windshield washer fluid, and fuel per owner's manual

### Seat Belt Use
- Fasten both lap and shoulder belts on every trip
- "Single most effective thing you can do to reduce the risk of death or injury"
- 2017 PA data: 80.2% of crash-involved people wore seat belts
- Drivers ages 16–24: highest number of unbuckled injuries/fatalities, lowest seat belt use overall

### Mirrors
- Rearview mirror: set to see traffic directly behind
- Side mirrors: position so you barely see your vehicle's side when leaning left/right
- Illegal to hang objects from rearview mirror while moving; tags/permits/placards only when parked

### Windshield
- Illegal to place anything on front windshield blocking view of roadway

### Head Restraints
- Top of head restraint must be at least as high as top of your ears
- Prevents/reduces neck injury severity during crashes

### Door Locks
- Lock doors: being ejected from vehicle increases death chances by 75%
- Reduces carjacking risk at intersections and parking lots

### Rearview Mirror
- Illegal to hang objects from rearview mirror while moving

## Sharing the Road

### Blind Pedestrians (White Cane / Guide Dog)
- Must slow down, always yield, proceed with caution, be prepared to stop

### School Buses
- Amber/yellow flashing: bus is preparing to stop — slow down
- Red lights flashing + stop arm extended: STOP at least 10 feet away
- Stop whether behind OR approaching from opposite direction
- Exception: divided highway with physical barrier (concrete/metal/guardrails/trees) — reduce speed, proceed with caution
- Penalty for failure to stop: 60-day license suspension + 5 points + fine

### Emergency Vehicles (Fire, Police, Ambulance, etc.)
- Pull to curb/side, stop, drive parallel to curb as near as possible
- On one-way street: drive to nearest roadside and stop
- Stay clear of intersections
- After emergency vehicle passes: maintain at least 500 feet distance
- Ensure no other emergency vehicle approaching before resuming

### Steer Clear Law (effective September 8, 2006)
- Move into non-adjacent lane when passing emergency response area (police, fire, EMS, towing, highway construction/maintenance)
- If unable to move over: reduce speed
- Penalties (summary offense):
  - 1st offense: up to $250 fine
  - 2nd offense: $500 fine
  - 3rd+ offense: $1,000 fine
  - Work zone violations: fines doubled
  - If serious injury/death: 90-day suspension + up to $1,000 fine
  - If highway worker/emergency responder: up to 1-year suspension + up to $10,000 fine

### Bicyclists
- Minimum 4 feet clearance when passing
- May cross yellow center lines to maintain 4-foot clearance if safe
- Cannot force bicyclists off road (may result in criminal charges)
- Dutch Reach: use far hand to open door, check mirrors for approaching cyclists

### Large Trucks — Blind Spots (No-Zones)
- Four major blind spots: front (~20 ft), rear (~30 ft), right side (2 lanes diagonal), left side (1 lane diagonal)
- Rule: if you can't see driver in side mirrors, they can't see you
- Loaded truck at 65 mph needs minimum 600 feet to stop; passenger car needs ~400 feet

### Truck Turns
- Trucks make wide right turns; may use adjacent lane space
- Don't squeeze between truck and curb

### Passengers in Open-Bed Trucks
- Maximum speed: 35 mph with any person in truck bed
- No persons under 18 in open truck bed (exceptions: farmer's child, farm labor child, hunting license holder during season, sanctioned parade)

### Funeral Processions
- Yield to procession vehicles; once lead vehicle clears intersection, rest may proceed
- May proceed through red light if lead vehicle started through on green
- Must yield to emergency vehicles
- May display flashing/revolving purple light
- Don't cut in/out unless directed by police or funeral director

### Seat Belts — PA Law
- All drivers and front-seat passengers must wear seat belts
- Passengers ages 8–17 must wear seat belts regardless of seating position
- At 30 mph, impact = falling from 3-story building; fatal at as low as 12 mph

### Child Restraints (Passenger Protection Act 229)
- Under 4: federally-approved child safety seat (rear-facing until outgrowing manufacturer limits)
- Ages 4–7: booster seat with lap and shoulder belts
- Ages 8–17: seat belt, any position
- NEVER place rear-facing seat in front seat with active airbag

### Airbags
- Maintain at least 10 inches from steering wheel
- Hold wheel at 9 & 3 or 8 & 4 o'clock (thumbs along wheel face, not inside rim)
- Children 12 and under: ride in rear seats

### Road Rage
- ~60% of ~1,500 PA highway deaths per year attributed to aggressive driving
- Never retaliate; don't try to teach a lesson
- If pursued: drive to police station, convenience store, or public location
