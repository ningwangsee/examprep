# Chapter 3d: Driver Factors
Source: https://www.pa.gov/agencies/dmv/driver-services/pennsylvania-drivers-manual/online-drivers-manual/driver-factors

## Inattention / Distractions
- Distractions account for 25–30% of police-reported crashes (~1.2 million annually)
- Undistracted driver misses important events 3% of the time
- Adult dialing cellphone misses same event 13% of the time
- Teen dialing cellphone misses same event 53% of the time
- Hands-free cellphone use still impairs driving ability
- Headphones/earpieces: may only be used in ONE ear for communication
- Teen drivers with teen passengers are MORE likely to have a crash than driving alone or with adults

## Distracting Activities
- Eating, drinking, smoking
- Adjusting audio devices (radio, CD, MP3)
- Cellphone use (hands-free or not)
- Passenger interaction
- Object manipulation (searching for items)
- Reading, writing, or texting
- Personal grooming (hair, makeup)
- Rubbernecking (watching crash scenes, work zones)
- Off-road observation

## Fatigue / Lack of Sleep
- Driving while fatigued has similar effects as driving under influence of alcohol
- At least 100,000 crashes annually caused by falling asleep while driving
- 1,500 deaths and 40,000 injuries result from drowsy driving crashes
- Nearly half of drowsy driving crashes involve drivers aged 15–24
- Teens sleeping fewer than 8 hours face increased crash risk
- Impairment equivalencies:
  - 18 hours awake ≈ BAC of 0.05%
  - 24 hours awake ≈ BAC of 0.10%

## Health Factors
- Eye exams recommended every 1–2 years
- Peripheral vision, distance judgment, and low-light vision can deteriorate
- Hearing detects dangers out of sight (vehicles in blind spots, emergency vehicles, trains)
- PA physicians must report to PennDOT individuals diagnosed with conditions that could impair safe driving
- Most dangerous medical conditions: seizure disorders (loss of consciousness), diabetes, heart conditions

## Alcohol and Driving
- ~40% of all traffic deaths involve drinking drivers
- PA: 30% of drivers aged 16–20 who died in MVC had been drinking
- Legal limits:
  - Under 21: BAC of 0.02% or more = DUI
  - Adults (21+): BAC of 0.08% = DUI
  - Commercial drivers (CDL): BAC of 0.04% = DUI
- Drivers with BAC of 0.04% are 2–7x more likely to crash than sober drivers
- Standard drink: 1.5 oz 80-proof liquor, 12 oz regular beer, or 5 oz wine
- Body processes roughly one drink per hour
- Only TIME reduces BAC; coffee/cold showers do NOT lower BAC
- A 110-lb female reaches 0.03% BAC after 8 oz of light beer
- A 140-lb male reaches 0.025% BAC after one 12-oz bottle of light beer
- U.S.: one person dies every half hour, one injured every 2 minutes from drunk driving

## Drugs and Driving
- Drugs (other than alcohol) involved in ~20% of motorist deaths annually
- Nearly 1 in 4 drivers tested positive for at least one impairing drug (NHTSA 2013–2014)
- 43% of drivers killed in crashes tested positive for drugs; 37% for alcohol
- Combining drugs and alcohol amplifies effects
- Drug effects on driving:
  - Marijuana: slows reaction, impairs time/distance judgment, decreases coordination
  - Cocaine/methamphetamine: aggressive and reckless driving
  - Sedatives: dizziness and drowsiness
  - Opioids: drowsiness, slow reaction time
  - OTC drugs (antihistamines, cough medicines, sleeping pills, decongestants): variable impairment
- "Don't operate heavy machinery" on labels = includes vehicles
- PA Get Help Now helpline: 1-800-662-HELP (4357), available 24/7
