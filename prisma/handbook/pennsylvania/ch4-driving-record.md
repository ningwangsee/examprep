# Chapter 4: Driving Record Information (Point System)
Source: https://www.pa.gov/agencies/dmv/driver-services/pennsylvania-drivers-manual/online-drivers-manual/pennsylvanias-point-system

## Point System Overview
- Points accumulate when found guilty of traffic violations
- Department acts when record reaches 6+ points

## Point Accumulation Remedial Actions
- 1st accumulation (6+ pts): choose between written Special Point Examination (removes 2 pts) OR Driver Improvement School (removes 4 pts)
- 2nd+ accumulation (6+ pts): must attend BOTH Departmental Hearing AND Driver Improvement School
  - 2nd accumulation: 15-day suspension
  - 3rd+ accumulation: 30-day suspension
  - After completion: 2 points removed
- Failure to attend hearing/comply with school = INDEFINITE suspension

## Junior Drivers (Under 18) — Automatic Suspensions
- 1st offense (6+ pts or 26+ mph over limit): 90-day suspension
- Additional occurrences: 120-day suspension

## Point Removal for Safe Driving
- 3 points removed per 12 consecutive months without suspensions/revocations/violations
- Once record reaches 0 points and stays 0 for 12 months: future accumulation restarts as "first accumulation"

## Post-Suspension Restoration
- After restoration: driving record shows 5 points (with exceptions)

## License Suspensions / Revocations (Partial List)
- Operating under influence (DUI)
- Homicide by vehicle
- Reckless driving / racing on highways
- Fleeing police
- Driving with suspended/revoked license
- Hit-and-run
- Failure to stop for school bus red lights: **60-day suspension**
- Noncompliance with railroad crossing barriers: **30-day suspension**
- Second+ unlicensed driving within 5 years

## Driving Without Insurance
- Must have continuous automobile liability insurance
- If uninsured 31+ days:
  - Vehicle registration suspended for 3 months
  - Driving privilege suspended for 3 months
  - Restoration requires proof of current insurance + applicable fees

## Traffic Stop Procedures
- Signal and pull safely to right edge; turn off engine and radio; lower window
- Activate interior lights at night before officer approaches
- Keep hands on steering wheel
- Blue-light-only vehicles are NOT police; red + blue or red-only = law enforcement
- May drive to well-lit area while acknowledging stop through flashers + reduced speed

## Crash Procedures
- Stop at or near scene
- Call police if: anyone injured/deceased, or vehicle requires towing
- Collect: other driver(s) names, addresses, phones, registration, insurance info
- Parked vehicle/property damage: locate owner or leave visible note; call police
- If police don't investigate but injury/death/towing needed: submit Driver's Accident Report Form within 5 days

## Fleeing or Eluding Police
- ~450 crashes annually from flee/elude attempts
- Conviction: 1-year driving privilege suspension + $500 fine + court costs + possible imprisonment

## DUI Laws

### Legal BAC Limits
- Adults (21+): 0.08% = DUI
- Under 21: 0.02% = DUI (zero tolerance)
- Commercial/CDL: 0.04% = DUI
- School bus drivers: 0.02% = DUI

### Implied Consent (Chemical Testing)
- By obtaining PA driver's license, consent to chemical testing (breath or blood)
- Refusal = automatic 1-year driving privilege suspension (even without DUI conviction)
- Refusal + DUI conviction: 2.5 years total suspension (or 3 years if prior DUI or prior refusal)
- Chemical test refusal restoration fees: 1st = $653; 2nd = $1,309; 3rd+ = $2,616

### DUI Penalty Tables

**Table 1: General Impairment (.08–.099 BAC)** (drivers 21+)
| | 1st Offense | 2nd Offense | 3rd Offense |
|--|------------|------------|------------|
| Sentence | 6 months probation | 5 days–6 months prison | 10 days–2 years prison |
| Fine | $300 | $300–$2,500 | $500–$5,000 |
| License | No action | 12-month suspension + 1-yr Ignition Interlock | 12-month suspension + 1-yr Ignition Interlock |

**Table 2: High Rate (.10–.159 BAC)**
Also applies to: under 21 with .02+ BAC; commercial drivers .04+ BAC; school bus drivers .02+ BAC; .08–.099 BAC if crash with serious injury/death/property damage
| | 1st Offense | 2nd Offense | 3rd Offense |
|--|------------|------------|------------|
| Sentence | 2 days–6 months prison | 30 days–6 months prison | 90 days–5 years prison |
| Fine | $500–$5,000 | $750–$5,000 | $1,500–$10,000 |
| License | 12-month suspension + 1-yr Ignition Interlock | 12-month suspension + 1-yr Interlock | 18-month suspension + 1-yr Interlock |

**Table 3: Highest Rate (.16+ BAC)**
Also applies to: test refusals at any BAC; Schedule I/II/III controlled substances (not prescribed); drug + alcohol combinations; inhalants
| | 1st Offense | 2nd Offense | 3rd Offense |
|--|------------|------------|------------|
| Sentence | 3 days–6 months prison | 90 days–5 years prison | 1–5 years prison |
| Fine | $1,000–$5,000 | $1,500 minimum | $2,500 minimum |
| License | 12-month suspension + 1-yr Ignition Interlock | 18-month suspension + 1-yr Interlock | 18-month suspension + 1-yr Interlock |

### Special DUI Circumstances
- Under 21 with any DUI → sentenced per High Rate table (2 days–6 months, 12-month suspension, $500–$5,000)
- Crash with serious injury/death/property damage → High Rate table
- Test refusal at any BAC → Highest Rate table
- All levels require Alcohol Highway Safety School (1st and 2nd offenses)

### Accelerated Rehabilitative Disposition (ARD)
- Available for first-time offenders at High and Highest impairment rates
- License loss: up to 90 days
- Court supervision: 6 months
- Fine: $300–$5,000 plus costs
- Must complete alcohol/drug evaluation + treatment if necessary
- Must complete 12.5 hours Alcohol Highway Safety School

## Underage Drinking (Under 21)
- Illegal to buy, consume, possess, or transport alcohol
- Minimum penalty: $500 fine + court costs
- Courts may require evaluation + mandatory alcohol education

## Adult Supplying Alcohol to Minors
- 1st minor: minimum $1,000 fine
- Each additional minor: minimum $2,500 each
- Up to 1 year jail
- Example: 17 minors at party = $41,000 ($1,000 + 16 × $2,500)
- Adults liable for guests consuming in their homes

## Driver's License Compact
- PA and member states share traffic violations across jurisdictions
- All traffic convictions reported to home state
- Out-of-state offenses treated as in-state offenses
- Non-response to out-of-state citation = indefinite PA suspension
