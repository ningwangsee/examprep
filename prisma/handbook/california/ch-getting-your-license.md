# Getting an Instruction Permit and Driver's License

Source: https://www.dmv.ca.gov/portal/handbook/california-driver-handbook/getting-an-instruction-permit-and-drivers-license/

---

## What You Need

To apply for an instruction permit or driver's license, applicants must provide:

- **Proof of identity** — "Proving who you are"
- **Two proofs of residency** — "Proving you live in California. Exceptions may apply"
- **Legal full name document** — for name discrepancies between identity documents
- **Social security number** — with possible exceptions

REAL ID requirements and acceptable documents are available at dmv.ca.gov/realid.

---

## Instruction Permit Requirements

### Application Steps for Class C Permits

Applicants must:
1. Complete a Driver's License & ID Card Application
2. Provide required documents
3. Pay a non-refundable application fee
4. Pass your knowledge test(s)
5. Pass a vision test

### Age Requirements (Under 18)

- Minimum age: **15½ years old**
- Complete a driver education program
- Parental/guardian approval required (both parents if joint custody applies)
- Must wait to use the instruction permit until starting behind-the-wheel driver training

---

## Driver's License Requirements

### General Requirements

- Practice with a California-licensed driver (minimum 18 years old; 25 years old if supervising a minor)
- Pass a behind-the-wheel drive test

### Under 18 Requirements

- Minimum age: **16 years old** to apply for a license
- Must have an instruction permit from California or another state for **at least 6 months** (or turn 18)
- Complete both driver education AND driver training
- Practice driving for at least **50 hours** with a California-licensed driver who is at least 25 years old
  - **10 of those 50 hours must be at night**

---

## Provisional License Restrictions

Drivers under 18 receive a **provisional license** with the following restrictions:

### Driving Hours Restriction
- **Cannot drive between 11 p.m. and 5 a.m.** during the first 12 months

### Passenger Restriction
- **Cannot drive with passengers under 20** (unless accompanied by a parent/guardian or a licensed adult 25 or older)

### Commercial Use
- Cannot drive for commercial purposes or operate commercial vehicles

### Exceptions (with documentation)
- **Medical needs** — physician's note required
- **School-related activities** — school principal's note required
- **Employment** — employer's note required
- **Transporting immediate family members** — parent/guardian's note required

Parents or guardians may cancel their teen's license using the "Request for Cancellation or Surrender of a Driver License or ID Card" form.

---

## Class C License

- A **Class C license** covers operation of standard passenger vehicles (cars, pickups, vans, SUVs)
- Covers vehicles with a Gross Vehicle Weight Rating (GVWR) of **26,000 lbs or less**
- Also covers towing a single vehicle with GVWR of 10,000 lbs or less (with combined GVWR under 26,000 lbs)

---

## REAL ID

- A REAL ID-compliant driver's license or ID is required to board domestic flights and enter certain federal facilities starting May 7, 2025
- Requires additional proof-of-identity documents (birth certificate or passport, Social Security card, two proofs of CA residency)
- Available at DMV offices; designated by a gold bear/star on the card

---

## License Renewal

- California licenses are generally valid for **5 years**
- Renewal can be done online, by mail, or in person
- DMV sends a renewal notice before expiration
- You must pass a vision test at renewal (written/drive test not usually required for renewal)
- Drivers age 70+ must renew in person

---

## Knowledge Test

- The knowledge test covers California traffic laws, signs, and safe driving practices
- Minimum passing score: **83%** (38 out of 46 questions — same as the official DMV test)
- Applicants under 18 must pass the test to receive an instruction permit
- Test is available in multiple languages at DMV offices
