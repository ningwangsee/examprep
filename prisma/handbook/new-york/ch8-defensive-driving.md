# Chapter 8: Defensive Driving
Source: https://dmv.ny.gov/new-york-state-drivers-manual-and-practice-tests/chapter-8-defensive-driving/

## Core Defensive Driving Principles
- Sit in vertical position with both hands on steering wheel
- Scan road continuously; don't focus only on vehicle ahead
- Check mirrors every few seconds
- Anticipate errors by other drivers, cyclists, pedestrians
- Never assume others will stop at signs

## Driver Distractions (Prohibited While Driving)
- Cell phone use or texting
- Arguments and emotional conversations
- Eating
- Unsecured children or pets

## Aggressive Driving
Includes: speeding, tailgating, unsafe lane changes without signaling, passing on unpaved shoulders, running stop signs/red lights, passing stopped school buses, impaired driving, reckless operation.

**Response to aggressive drivers:**
- Avoid eye contact; stay calm; move away safely
- Don't match their speed or challenge them
- Wear seat belt; ignore gestures/shouts
- Report to law enforcement with vehicle description, plate, location, direction
- If followed: drive to nearest police station; never leave your vehicle

## Road Rage
- Definition: "an angry, hostile state which can increase into violent criminal actions...that result from the operation of a motor vehicle"
- Key distinction: Aggressive driving violates traffic laws; road rage involves criminal law violations
- Road Rage behaviors: shouts, excessive horn use, obscene gestures, threats, cutting off, blocking, chasing, deliberately ramming, assault
- Penalties: fines, imprisonment, probation, driver license revocation/suspension

## Speed Limits
- Default limit (no posted signs): 55 mph (88 km/h)
- New York City: 25 mph (40 km/h) unless otherwise posted
- Must obey posted limits; can be ticketed for driving too fast "for conditions"
- Some highways have minimum speed limits to maintain traffic flow
- Work zones: ticket fines double for speeding

## Following Distance (Space Cushion)
- Two-Second Rule: Select an object ahead; count "one thousand one, one thousand two" after vehicle ahead passes it; if you reach first, you're too close
- Bad weather/large trucks: increase to 3–4 seconds minimum
- Most common work zone crash: rear-end collisions
- If tailgated: move lanes if safe or reduce speed; don't brake suddenly

## Seat Belts & Child Restraints

**General Requirements:**
- New York is "primary enforcement" state — officers can ticket for non-compliance
- Applies to visitors from outside NY State
- Driver fine for non-compliance: up to $50
- Passenger age 16+ fine: up to $50 per person

**Driver Responsibility for Passengers Under 16:**
- Fine: $25–$100 per violation
- License points: 3 points per violation

**Child Restraint Requirements:**

| Age | Requirement |
|-----|-------------|
| Under 4 years | Federally approved child safety seat (rear-facing until age 2 or manufacturer's height/weight limit), attached by safety belt or LATCH system |
| 4–7 years | Appropriate federally approved booster seat with lap and shoulder belt |
| 8–15 years | Seat belt (one person per belt); continued booster seat recommended if under 4'9" or under 100 lbs |

**Seat Belt Positioning:**
- Lap belt across hips/lap; shoulder belt across chest and shoulder
- Never across arm or under armpit

**Air Bag Safety:**
- Work WITH seat belts, not replacing them
- Sit at least 10 inches from air bag cover
- Hands at 3 and 9 o'clock positions on steering wheel
- CRITICAL: Never place rear-facing infant seat in front seat with passenger air bag

**Exemptions from seat belt requirement:**
- Certain emergency vehicles
- Vehicles 1964 or older
- Bus passengers (except school buses)
- Taxi/livery passengers under 8
- Rural letter carriers performing duties
- Persons with physician-certified disabling conditions

## Work Zones
- Warning signs: orange diamond-shaped signs
- Decrease speed immediately; be alert to flaggers (have same authority as signs)
- Merge into correct lane when first notified (don't speed to end of closed lane)
- "Road Work 1,500 feet" = 17 seconds at 60 mph
- Work zones can be stationary or mobile
- Obey signs until "work zone ended" sign appears
- Speeding fines double in work zones

## Roundabouts
- Three Basic Principles:
  1. Yield-at-entry rule (approaching vehicles yield to circulating traffic)
  2. Low speeds (deflected/curved entrances restrict speed)
  3. Adequate deflection for safety (speeds restricted to 30 mph/50 km/h or less)
- Look left — roundabout traffic has right-of-way
- Enter when adequate gap exists in circulating traffic
- Signal right turn before exiting; yield to pedestrians when exiting
- Don't pass bicycles inside roundabout
- Proceed counterclockwise to exit

## Drowsy & Fatigued Driving

**Fatigue Symptoms:**
- Eyes close or lose focus; difficulty keeping head up; continuous yawning
- Wandering thoughts; don't remember driving last miles
- Drifting between lanes, tailgating, missing signs; jerking car back into lane; hitting rumble strips

**High-Risk Drivers:**
- Sleep-deprived, driving long distances, driving through night, taking sleepiness-increasing medications, driving alone

**Prevention:**
- Get adequate sleep before trips; drive long trips with companion
- Schedule stops every 100 miles or 2 hours
- Avoid alcohol and impairing medications
- Don't drive first 15 minutes after waking

**When Drowsy:**
- Find safe place to stop; pull into safe area away from traffic; take 15–45 minute nap
- Drink coffee/caffeine (takes ~30 minutes to enter bloodstream)
- Don't depend on radio or open window to stay awake

## Cellular/Mobile Phone Use

**Hand-Held Phone Law:**
- Traffic infraction to speak into or listen to hand-held mobile phone while driving
- Phone may be hand-held to activate, begin, or end call only
- First offense fine: up to $200
- License points: 5 points
- Applies to visitors from outside NY

**Hands-Free Phones:** Legal to use at any time while driving

**Texting & Electronic Devices:**
- Illegal to send/receive text messages or emails while driving
- First offense: up to $200 fine; Second offense (within 18 months): up to $250; Third+ offense: up to $450

**Probationary/Junior License Holders:**
- First conviction for hand-held phone or texting: 120-day suspension
- Subsequent conviction within 6 months of restoration: at least 1-year revocation

**Exemptions:** Emergency calls, law enforcement, fire department, authorized emergency vehicle operators

## Vehicle Maintenance
- Annual inspection required
- Minimum legal tire tread: 2/32nds inch (.16 cm)
- Penny test: place Lincoln's head first into groove; if entire head visible, tires too worn
- Sit at least 10 inches from air bag cover
