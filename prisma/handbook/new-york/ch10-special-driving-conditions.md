# Chapter 10: Special Driving Conditions
Source: https://dmv.ny.gov/new-york-state-drivers-manual-and-practice-tests/chapter-10-special-driving-conditions/

## Railroad Crossings
- A grade crossing is the point at which train tracks intersect with a roadway and is considered an intersection
- Expect trains from any direction at any time
- Never race trains, drive around lowered gates, or stop on tracks
- Must stop at least 15 feet from tracks

**Active Crossings (with signals/gates):**
- Do not proceed until gates are completely raised and lights are off
- Illegal and dangerous to cross with flashing lights or lowered gates

**Passive Crossings (no signals/gates):**
- Slow down; be prepared to stop; look both directions on all tracks before crossing
- "Trains cannot stop quickly"

**Multiple Tracks:**
- Wait until you can clearly see down all tracks in both directions before crossing

**Vehicles that must always stop at railroad crossings:**
- School buses (with or without passengers)
- Buses with passengers
- Vehicles with explosives or flammable cargo

**Stalling on Tracks — Emergency Procedure:**
1. Get everyone out immediately
2. Get away from tracks
3. Locate Emergency Notification System sign; call the number provided
4. Run toward train's direction at 45-degree angle away from tracks (never run along tracks)

## Expressway Driving
- Any divided highway where traffic moves in one direction on two or more lanes
- Speed limits: normally 55 mph (can be posted at 65 mph in rural areas)
- If you miss your exit: stay on expressway until next exit; never back up or cross median
- Signal at least 100 feet (30 m) before reaching exit ramp
- Decrease speed on exit ramp (lower speed limits often posted)

**Entering Expressway:**
- Unless STOP, YIELD, or traffic light present: use ramp to accelerate to expressway speed
- Signal and check over shoulder for existing traffic
- If entrance lane too short: stop and wait for large traffic gap, then enter and accelerate quickly

## Night Driving
- About 90% of driving decisions are based on what you see
- Drive slower than daytime, especially on unfamiliar or narrow curving roads
- Headlights cover about 350 feet ahead; drive within that range to stop safely

**Headlight Requirements:**
- Required from one-half hour after the sun sets to one-half hour before the sun rises
- Required when visibility is less than 1,000 feet
- Required when using windshield wipers for rain, snow, or sleet
- Turn on at dawn, dusk, and in fog
- Do not use parking lights or daytime running lights as replacement for headlights

**High Beam Rules:**
- Must use low beam when within 500 feet (150 m) of an oncoming vehicle
- Must use low beam when within 200 feet (60 m) of a vehicle ahead (even if in different lane)
- Dim lights for approaching pedestrians
- If approaching driver doesn't dim: flash high beams for one second, return to low beam

**Glare Management:**
- Switch inside rear-view mirror to "night" position to reduce glare
- Keep interior roof light off; dim dashboard lights; adjust visors

## Driving in Rain, Fog, or Snow

**General:**
- Even slight rain, snow, or ice makes roads slippery
- Wet leaves are hazardous
- NY State law requires headlights when weather requires windshield wipers
- Daytime running lights do not qualify as headlights
- Keep headlights on low beam (high beams reflect rain/fog/snow and reduce visibility)

**Hydroplaning:**
- Can cause complete loss of traction and control of steering
- Normally occurs at higher speeds; can occur with worn tread or improperly inflated tires
- Drive slowly in heavy rain; good tires with deep tread help prevent it

**Driving Techniques:**
- Reduce speed; signal turns further ahead; brake early when decreasing speed

**Fog Lights:**
- Front fog lights may be amber or white
- Back fog lights must be red
- Must be correctly installed and DMV-approved
- Switch off when visibility improves to reduce glare

## Winter Driving
- Metal studs allowed only from October 16 through April 30
- Never combine radial and non-radial tires on same vehicle
- Clear ice and snow from entire vehicle before driving: headlights, back lights, wipers, hood, roof, all windows

**Skid Recovery — Rear Wheels:**
- Turn steering wheel in direction vehicle is trying to go
- If rear slides left, steer left; if right, steer right
- ABS: keep even pressure on brake pedal
- No ABS: pump pedal carefully

**Skid Recovery — Front Wheels:**
- Take foot off gas and shift to neutral or push in clutch
- Do not try to steer immediately
- When traction returns, turn wheel in desired direction

**Braking on Slippery Roads:**
- Brake early, carefully, gently
- Press brakes in slow, steady strokes; allow wheels to keep turning
- If wheels begin to lock up, decrease brake pedal pressure

**Road Conditions:**
- Bridges, ramps, and overpasses freeze first during sleet/freezing rain/snow

## Deer Collision Avoidance
- Two-thirds of deer collisions occur in October, November, and December (breeding season)
- Highest daily activity: dawn and dusk
- If one deer crosses, expect more (deer travel in groups)
- Use emergency lights or flash headlights to warn other drivers when deer seen

## Driving Emergencies

**General Rule:** "Do not panic" — you'll have a second or two to think before acting

**Tire Blowout:**
- Hold steering wheel tightly; slowly take foot off gas pedal
- Handle skid as you would on ice/snow; do not brake until vehicle is controlled
- Leave road when safe

**Steering Failure:**
- Slowly take foot off gas; turn on emergency lights
- Keep foot off brake while safe; brake very carefully as vehicle decreases speed

**Brake Failure:**
- Try pumping brake pedal; if unsuccessful, use emergency/parking brake gradually
- Shifting to lower gear helps decrease speed

**Stuck Gas Pedal:**
- Try hooking shoe under pedal; if unsuccessful, shift into neutral
- Use brake to slow vehicle; do not turn off ignition (could lose steering control)

**Running Off Pavement:**
- Do not suddenly steer or swerve back onto pavement
- Ease foot off gas; brake gently; when speed decreases, turn carefully back onto pavement

**Vehicle Approaching Head-On:**
- Decrease speed; pull over to right; sound horn
- Do not turn into left lane (other driver may recover and cause head-on)

**Going into Water:**
- Vehicle normally floats temporarily; release seat belt and escape through window
- If vehicle sinks: get into back seat (air pocket may form); when vehicle settles, escape through window

**Fire:**
- Pull off road; park; turn off ignition; get away from car; call fire department
- Do not try to fight fire yourself
