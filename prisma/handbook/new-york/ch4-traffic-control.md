# Chapter 4: Traffic Control
Source: https://dmv.ny.gov/new-york-state-drivers-manual-and-practice-tests/chapter-4-traffic-control/

## Traffic Sign Categories & Colors
- **Regulation Signs:** White rectangles with black letters; some use different shapes or red symbols
- **Warning Signs:** Yellow diamonds with black letters/symbols
- **Destination Signs:** Green with white letters
- **Service Signs:** Blue with white letters/symbols
- **Work Area Signs:** Orange with black letters/symbols

## Regulation Signs — Specific Rules

**Stop Sign (red with white letters):**
- Come to a full stop; yield right-of-way to vehicles and pedestrians
- Must stop before stop line, crosswalk, or at intersection point providing clear traffic view

**Yield Sign (red and white):**
- Decrease speed, prepare to stop, yield right-of-way
- Full stop required if traffic conditions demand it

## Work Zone Signs
- Speed limits as low as 25 MPH may be posted
- Obey flag persons' signals:
  - Stop: flag person holds stop sign or extends flag horizontally
  - Proceed: flag person gestures forward
  - Slow: flag person waves flag up and down

## Traffic Light Rules

**Steady Red Light:**
- Stop completely; do not proceed until green
- Right turn permitted after full stop, yielding to oncoming traffic and pedestrians
- Left turn allowed from one-way street to another one-way street after full stop and yielding
- Cannot turn on red if "NO TURN ON RED" sign posted
- **New York City exception:** No right turn on red unless sign permits
- School buses carrying pupils cannot turn right on any red light

**Flashing Red Light:**
- Same as STOP sign: stop, yield right-of-way, proceed when safe

**Red Arrow:**
- Do not proceed in arrow direction; right or left turn on red arrow is prohibited

**Steady Yellow Light:**
- Light will change to red; prepare to stop

**Flashing Yellow Light:**
- Drive with caution; no stop required

**Yellow Arrow:**
- Green arrow protection ending; prepare to stop if turning in that direction

**Steady Green Light:**
- Proceed while yielding right-of-way to other traffic as legally required

**Green Arrow:**
- Proceed in arrow direction while yielding right-of-way as required by law

**Traffic Light Failure Protocol:**
- Stop as you would for a STOP sign, then proceed by right-of-way rules unless directed by traffic officer

## Lane Use Control Lights
- Red "X": Do not enter this lane
- Yellow "X": Move from this lane
- Flashing Yellow "X": Lane reserved for left turns only
- Green Arrow: Lane available for use

## Pavement Markings

**Line Colors:**
- White lines separate same-direction traffic
- Yellow lines separate opposite-direction traffic
- Edge lines mark where travel lane ends and shoulder begins
- Driving across edge line is illegal except by police direction

**Line Types:**
- One Broken Line: Safe to pass or change lanes if done safely
- Solid Line with Broken Line:
  - Solid side: Cannot pass or cross except to make left turn into driveway
  - Broken side: Can pass if safe
- Double Solid Lines: Cannot pass or change lanes; crossing prohibited except to turn left into/from highway
- One Solid Line: Can pass only when road obstructions or traffic conditions make it necessary

**Stop & Crosswalk Lines:**
- White stop line marks where vehicle must stop
- Parallel white lines form crosswalk
- Must stop before reaching stop line or crosswalk when required by sign, light, or officer

**Arrow Markings:**
- Show required lanes for specific directions; must be in correct lane before solid line separating lanes

**Diamond Symbols:**
- Indicate reserved lanes for buses, HOV vehicles, bicycles, or special vehicles
- Cannot enter unless vehicle meets requirements shown by signs during specified times

## Traffic Officers
- "Directions given by traffic officers take precedence over signs, signals or pavement markings"
- Authorized traffic directors: police officers, fire police, highway work area flag persons, school crossing personnel, school bus drivers
- When multiple controls conflict, obey in this order: traffic officer directives supersede all other traffic control devices

## Sign Shapes (Key Associations)
- Octagon (8 sides): STOP
- Triangle (inverted): YIELD
- Diamond: Warning
- Rectangle: Regulatory or informational
- Pentagon (5 sides): School zone/crossing
- Circle: Railroad crossing advance warning
