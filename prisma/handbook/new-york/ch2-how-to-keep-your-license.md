# Chapter 2: How to Keep Your License
Source: https://dmv.ny.gov/new-york-state-drivers-manual-and-practice-tests/chapter-2-how-to-keep-your-license/

## Suspension vs. Revocation
- **Suspension:** Driving privilege temporarily removed for a set period, then restored. May require $50 suspension termination fee.
- **Revocation:** License canceled permanently. Must reapply after revocation period ends and pay $100 re-application fee. Application may be denied based on driving record.

## Junior Permit/License Holders
- 60-day suspension for: one violation worth 3+ points OR two other violations
- 60-day revocation for: serious violation (3+ points) or two violations within 6 months after restoration
- 120-day suspension for: texting or cell phone violation
- Junior permit requires minimum 6-month holding period (excluding suspension/revocation time) before road test

## Probation for Adult New Drivers (Age 18+)
- 6-month probation period applies to drivers 18+ passing road test or obtaining license after revocation
- 60-day suspension for: DWAI (alcohol), speeding, reckless driving, following too closely, speed contest, or any two traffic violations during probation
- New 6-month probation begins after suspension ends
- License revocation (minimum 6 months) if convicted of covered violations or any two traffic violations during second probation period
- 120-day suspension for: texting or cell phone violation during probation

## Traffic Tickets
- Follow ticket instructions immediately or face default conviction and indefinite license suspension
- NY won't record most out-of-state moving violations (except Canada)
- License suspended if ticket from any state (except Alaska, California, Michigan, Montana, Oregon, Virginia, Wisconsin) goes unanswered
- Over 21 convicted of out-of-state alcohol/drug violation: minimum 90-day revocation
- Under 21 with any alcohol/drug violation: minimum 1-year revocation or until age 21 (whichever longer)

## Mandatory Suspension/Revocation — Alcohol & Drugs

| Violation | Penalty |
|-----------|---------|
| Aggravated DWI (.18 BAC) | Minimum 1-year revocation |
| DWI (.08 BAC) | Minimum 6-month revocation |
| DWAI (alcohol) | 90-day suspension |
| DWAI-drug | Minimum 6-month revocation |
| Out-of-state DUI | 90-day to 6-month revocation |
| Chemical test refusal (21+) | Minimum 1-year revocation |
| Chemical test refusal (21+, within 5 years of prior) | Minimum 18-month revocation |
| Chemical test refusal (under 21, first) | Minimum 1-year revocation |
| Chemical test refusal (under 21, second) | Minimum 1-year or until age 21 (whichever longer) |
| Zero Tolerance test refusal | Minimum 1-year revocation |

## Under Age 21 Violations
- Any alcohol/drug violation while under 21: minimum 1-year revocation
- Zero Tolerance Law: BAC .02-.07 results in 6-month suspension
- Second Zero Tolerance violation: 1-year revocation or until age 21 (whichever longer)

## Speeding & Other Mandatory Revocations (Minimum 6 Months)
- Three speeding and/or misdemeanor traffic violations within 18 months
- Three "passing stopped school bus" violations within 3 years
- One "leaving scene of personal injury/fatal crash" violation
- One "speed contest" violation (second within 12 months = minimum 1-year revocation)

## Point System
- Convictions dated from violation date, not conviction date
- 11+ points within 24 months: License suspended by mail notice
- Point reduction: Up to 4 points through DMV-approved Motor Vehicle Crash Prevention Course; saves up to 10% insurance premium

## Fees & Civil Penalties
- Suspension Termination Fee: $50 (non-refundable)
- Zero Tolerance Law Suspension: $125 civil penalty + $100 suspension termination fee
- License Re-application Fee: $100 (non-refundable) after revocation
- Chemical test refusal: $500 civil penalty ($550 if commercial vehicle)
- Chemical test refusal within 5 years of prior: $750

## Driver Responsibility Assessment
- For Agg-DWI, DWI, DWAI, DWAI-drug, or chemical test refusal: $250 per year for 3 years
- For 6+ points in 18-month period: $100 per year for 3 years, plus $25 per additional point per year for 3 years

## School Bus Stop-Arm Camera Violations (Vehicle Owner)
- First violation: $250
- Second violation (within 18 months): $275
- Third+ violation (within 18 months): $300

## Driving While Suspended/Revoked
- Criminal offense
- Fines: $200–$5,000
- Mandatory imprisonment or probation
- Vehicle seizure/forfeiture possible
- Criminal charge for drivers with 20+ suspensions for failure to answer tickets
