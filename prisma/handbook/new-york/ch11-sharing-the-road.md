# Chapter 11: Sharing the Road
Source: https://dmv.ny.gov/new-york-state-drivers-manual-and-practice-tests/chapter-11-sharing-the-road/

## Pedestrians

**Right-of-Way Rules:**
- Pedestrians crossing at marked or unmarked crossings have right-of-way
- Drivers must decrease speed or stop completely
- Blind pedestrians with guide dogs or white/metal canes ALWAYS have right-of-way at crosswalks

**Pedestrian Legal Requirements:**
- Must obey traffic signals and signs
- Must use sidewalks when available
- Must face traffic when walking on road edge
- Cannot stand in road to hitchhike or conduct business

**Driver Responsibilities:**
- Check behind vehicle before backing, especially for small children
- Look all directions before turning
- Never pass vehicles stopped at crosswalks
- Do not block or park in crosswalks
- Always stop for school buses with flashing red lights

## Bicyclists

**Bicycle Equipment Requirements:**
- Ages 1–13: approved helmet mandatory
- Adults: follow local helmet laws
- Must have adequate brakes and horn/bell audible 100 feet away
- Night use: headlight visible 500 feet ahead, red taillight visible 300 feet behind
- Dealer bicycles: wide-angle spoke reflectors, rear reflector, pedal reflectors

**Bicyclist Operational Rules:**
- Must ride in bicycle lane if available
- Without lane: remain near right curb/edge or right shoulder
- Never more than two side-by-side per lane
- Cannot ride on sidewalk if prohibited locally
- Full stop before entering roadway from driveway/alley
- Signal turns and stops with hand signals
- Keep one hand on handlebars always
- Never carry infant under one year
- Ages 1–4: must use attached safety seat

**Crash Reporting:**
- Must report crashes causing death or serious injury to DMV within 10 days (Form MV-104C)

**Driver Precautions with Bicyclists:**
- Check blind spots before turning or parking; do not pass too closely; reduce speed when passing
- Look for bike lanes, 2-way cycle tracks, bike boxes, green painted lanes
- Yield right-of-way as to other vehicles

## Motorcycles

**Visibility Issues:**
- Smaller size makes distance/speed difficult to judge
- 41% of fatal US crashes involve "other vehicle turning left"
- 53.6% of reported NY State crashes involve another vehicle

**Driver Safety Practices:**
- Do not tailgate; motorcycles stop shorter than cars
- Give extra space in rain or darkness
- Be careful when passing; air pressure affects stability
- Look actively for motorcycles, not just cars/trucks

**Helmet Requirements:**
- Approved USDOT FMVSS 218 helmet mandatory for motorcyclists
- At least 4 square inches reflective material per side recommended
- Approved eye protection mandatory, even with windshield

## Moped Operators (Limited Use Motorcycles)

| Class | Speed | Lane | Helmet |
|-------|-------|------|--------|
| Class A (31–40 mph) | Any traffic lane | Required |
| Class B (21–30 mph) | Right lane only, except left turns | Required |
| Class C (20 mph or less) | Right lane only, except left turns | Recommended |

## Large Vehicles (Trucks, Buses, RVs)

**Blind Spot Zones ("No Zones"):**
- Front: up to 20 feet ahead of tractor
- Behind: deep blind spots directly to rear
- Sides: much larger than cars on both sides

**Stopping Distance at 55 mph:**
- Passenger car: 193 feet
- Tractor-trailer (cool brakes): 256 feet
- Tractor-trailer (hot brakes): 430 feet
- Tractor-trailer (empty): 249 feet

**Driver Safety:**
- Never cut abruptly in front; allow extra seconds when exiting
- When passing: see entire front of vehicle in mirror before returning to lane
- Never pass truck backing up; wait for maneuver completion
- Leave space when stopped behind truck, especially uphill
- Don't underestimate size/speed of approaching trucks

## Slow-Moving Vehicles
- Display reflective orange triangle on back
- Used on animal-drawn vehicles, farm equipment, construction vehicles, rural mail trucks

## All-Terrain Vehicles & Snowmobiles
- May legally cross state highways and local roads
- Must stop and yield to traffic before crossing; must cross at 90-degree angle to road surface

## Horse Riders
- Must ride single file near right curb/edge or right shoulder
- Driver must approach at acceptable speed and distance
- **Illegal to sound horn when approaching or passing horse**

## Crash Statistics
- 60%+ of fatal car-truck crashes: car driver contributes to cause
- 80% of these crashes: car driver is killed
- Four of five times: car driver is killed in car-truck crashes

## Crash Procedures
- Must report crashes causing death or serious injury to DMV within 10 days
- Form: MV-104C available at motor vehicle offices or online
- Leaving the scene of a crash involving personal injury or death: mandatory revocation (minimum 1 year)
