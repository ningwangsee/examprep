# Chapter 5: Intersections and Turns
Source: https://dmv.ny.gov/new-york-state-drivers-manual-and-practice-tests/chapter-5-intersections-and-turns/

## Right-of-Way Rules

**At Intersections with Traffic Controls:**
- A driver who approaches an intersection must yield to traffic already in the intersection
- A green light does not override this rule if another vehicle is already crossing

**Opposite Direction Traffic:**
- If drivers approaching from opposite directions reach an intersection at about the same time, a driver that turns left must yield to traffic that moves straight or turns right
- Left-turning drivers must yield to all oncoming traffic that poses a hazard, even without signs or signals

**Uncontrolled Intersections:**
- Two drivers reach intersection simultaneously at right angles: driver on the left must yield to the driver on the right

**Driveway/Private Property:**
- A vehicle entering a roadway from a driveway, alley, private road, or another place that is not a roadway must stop and yield to traffic on the roadway and to pedestrians

**Pedestrian Crossings:**
- Drivers must yield to pedestrians who legally use marked or unmarked crosswalks
- Drivers must yield before turning into crosswalks on their left or right

**Blocking Intersections (Gridlock Prevention):**
- Drivers cannot enter intersections if backed-up traffic prevents complete passage

**Traffic Circles/Rotaries:**
- A driver who approaches a traffic circle or rotary must yield to drivers already in the circle

## Emergency Vehicles

**General Requirements:**
- Yield to fire, ambulance, police, and authorized emergency vehicles with flashing red, red and blue, or red and white lights and sounding a siren or air-horn
- Safely pull over immediately to the right edge of the road and stop
- Must pull over even if emergency vehicle approaches in opposite lane on two-way roads
- If unable to locate emergency vehicle, pull over to right edge and stop until certain it's not approaching

**Emergency Vehicle Privileges:**
- Can legally exceed speed limits, pass red lights and STOP signs, travel wrong way on one-way streets, and make prohibited turns

**Move Over Law:**
- Reduce speed when encountering parked/stopped authorized emergency or hazard vehicles with activated emergency lights
- On parkways, interstates, and controlled-access roads with multiple lanes: move from adjacent lane unless unsafe
- Applies to vehicles with amber, blue, green, or combinations of blue and amber lights
- Violations are moving violations

## Blue, Green, and Amber Lights
- Blue lights: volunteer firefighters responding to alarms — NOT authorized emergency vehicles; must obey all traffic laws
- Green lights: volunteer ambulance or rescue squad members — NOT authorized emergency vehicles
- Amber lights: hazard vehicles (snow plows, tow trucks, disabled vehicle assistance vehicles, rural mail delivery, school buses) — drivers not required to yield but should as courtesy

## Signaling Requirements
- Law requires signaling a turn or lane change with turn lights or hand signals at least 100 feet (30 m) ahead
- Best practice: signal before braking or beginning the turn

**Hand Signals:**
- Left Turn: extend left arm horizontally out window
- Right Turn: extend left arm upward at 90-degree angle
- Stop/Slow Down: extend left arm downward at 90-degree angle, palm facing backward

## Right Turns
- Get as far to the right as possible; do not make wide, sweeping turns
- Turn into the right lane of the destination road unless signs direct otherwise
- Reduce speed before turning; check all directions for traffic
- Keep wheels straight until actually beginning turn
- Rear wheels travel inside the path of front wheels, closer to curb
- Watch for pedestrians, bicyclists, moped riders

## Left Turns — Specific Scenarios

**From One-Way Road into One-Way Road:**
- Move into left lane when preparing to turn; turn into left lane of destination road

**From One-Way Road into Two-Way Road:**
- Approach turn in left lane; enter destination road to right of center line but as close as possible to it

**From Two-Way Road into Two-Way Road:**
- Approach from right half of roadway closest to center
- Use left side of intersection to avoid interfering with oncoming left-turning traffic
- Keep to right of center line of destination road but as close as possible to it

**From Two-Way Road into One-Way Road:**
- Approach from right half of roadway closest to center
- Make turn before reaching intersection center; turn into left lane of destination road

**Protected Left Turn (Green Arrow):**
- Can make left turn with green arrow; must yield to other traffic

## U-Turns
- Avoid on highways unless absolutely necessary
- Make U-turns only from left portion of lane nearest to centerline; never from right lane
- Cannot make U-turn near hilltop, curve, or anywhere drivers cannot see vehicle from 500 feet (150 m) in either direction
- Illegal in NYC business districts and where NO U-TURN signs posted
- Never on limited-access expressways
- Prohibited in school zones

## Three-Point Turn
- Use on narrow, two-way streets unless prohibited
- Steps:
  1. Signal right, pull to right and stop; signal left, check traffic from all directions
  2. Turn left across road, stop facing left curb/edge
  3. Turn wheel fully right, look behind, back up before reaching right curb/obstacle
  4. Stop, check traffic, turn wheel fully left, pull forward when safe
