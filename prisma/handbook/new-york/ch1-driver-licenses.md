# Chapter 1: Driver Licenses
Source: https://dmv.ny.gov/new-york-state-drivers-manual-and-practice-tests/chapter-1-driver-licenses/

## License Types & Eligibility

### Operator, Class D
- Minimum age: 18 (or 17 with driver education certificate)
- Vehicles with GVWR ≤26,000 lbs; towing up to 10,000 lbs
- Covers Class B and C mopeds

### Junior Operator, Class DJ
- Minimum age: 16
- Vehicle limit: GVWR ≤10,000 lbs; towing ≤3,000 lbs
- "Limited with where, when, and with whom you can drive"

### Taxi/Livery, Class E
- Minimum age: 18
- Same vehicle ops as Class D; permits passenger transport (14 or fewer passengers) for hire

### Motorcycle, Class M
- Minimum age: 18 (or 17 with driver education certificate)

### Junior Motorcycle, Class MJ
- Minimum age: 16
- Restricted per graduated licensing laws

### Enhanced Driver License
- Valid for US/Canada/Mexico/Bermuda/Caribbean land and sea border crossings

### REAL ID License
- Required for domestic air travel beginning May 7, 2025
- Must be obtained in-person at DMV office

## Proof of Name (6+ Points Required)
- 6 pts: Current NY photo driver license/permit/non-driver ID (expired ≤2 years OK)
- 4 pts: Valid US passport or passport card; photo license from other US state/Canadian province (expired ≤2 years)
- 3 pts: Certificate of Citizenship/Naturalization; Permanent Resident ID (I-551); US military photo ID; NYS Medicaid card with photo
- 2 pts: NYS vehicle title/registration; marriage/divorce record; NYS professional license
- 1 pt: Valid US credit card; bank statements; utility bills; high school diploma/GED; W-2 forms

## Vision & Knowledge Tests
- Vision requirement: 20/40 vision in at least one eye with or without corrective lenses
- Written test: 20 questions total; must pass 14 correct; must answer ≥2 of 4 road sign questions correctly
- Available in multiple languages via touch screen or paper
- Online Knowledge Test Application (OKTA): for students age 15 years 11 months and older at participating high schools; offered in English and Spanish

## Learner Permit Requirements
- Valid for minimum 6 months before road test eligibility
- Supervising driver must be licensed, age ≥21, seated in front seat
- NYC requirement: vehicle must have dual controls

### Learner Permit Regional Restrictions

**New York City (5 Boroughs):**
- 5AM–9PM: Requires supervision with dual controls
- 9PM–5AM: No driving allowed

**Long Island (Nassau & Suffolk):**
- 5AM–9PM: Requires supervision with dual controls
- 9PM–5AM: No driving allowed

**Upstate (All Other Counties):**
- 5AM–9PM: Any licensed driver age ≥21
- 9PM–5AM: Parent, guardian, in loco parentis, driver education teacher, or driving school instructor only

### Junior Learner Permit Passenger Restrictions
- Cannot drive with more than one passenger under age 21 unless members of immediate family
- Front seat: supervising driver only
- All passengers must wear seatbelts; child passengers require appropriate restraints

## Road Test Requirements
- Recommended: ≥50 total hours practice; ≥15 hours after sunset; ≥10 hours in moderate-to-heavy traffic
- Safe Driving Course required before road test (5-hour Pre-Licensing course, or high school/college driver ed, or online course for age 18+)
- Certificate valid for 1 year
- DMV-certified Point & Insurance Reduction Program does NOT meet this requirement
- Junior drivers must submit Certification of Supervised Driving (MV-262) at road test

## Junior Operator License Restrictions

### New York City (5 Boroughs)
- No driving at any time (neither 5AM–9PM nor 9PM–5AM)

### Long Island (Nassau & Suffolk)
- 5AM–9PM: Alone only to employment, work-study, college/evening high school, driver education, farm employment; OR with licensed parent/guardian
- 9PM–5AM: Alone only to work-study, college/evening high school, driver education, farm employment

### Upstate (All Other Counties)
- 5AM–9PM: May drive without accompaniment
- 9PM–5AM: Alone only to employment or school course; otherwise requires parent/guardian/in loco parentis

### General Junior Driver Restrictions
- Cannot drive with more than one passenger under age 21 unless members of immediate family
- All passengers must wear seatbelts; child passengers require appropriate restraints
- Motorcyclists: no passengers except supervising driver

## License Renewal
- Renewal notice mailed ~45 days before expiration
- Can renew up to 1 year before expiration
- REAL ID conversion requires in-person visit with proofs of identity, Social Security Number, date of birth, US citizenship/lawful status, and two proofs of NY State residence (PO Box not acceptable)

## Address Changes
- Must notify DMV within 10 days
- Via mail using Change-of-Address Form (MV-232) or online through MyDMV

## New Residents
- Must apply for NY license within 30 days of establishing residency
- Must turn in out-of-state license
- Must pass vision test
- If out-of-state license valid <6 months OR expired >1 year ago: must also pass written and road tests
- Foreign country license holders: must pass vision test, complete safe driving course, pass written and road tests
- Under age 16: Cannot drive in NY regardless of out-of-state license

## Miscellaneous
- It is a crime to alter or forge any motor vehicle document
- Cannot hold NY and out-of-state licenses simultaneously
- Must have valid license to drive legally in NY State
