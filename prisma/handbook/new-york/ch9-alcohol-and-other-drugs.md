# Chapter 9: Alcohol and Other Drugs
Source: https://dmv.ny.gov/new-york-state-drivers-manual-and-practice-tests/chapter-9-alcohol-and-other-drugs/

## Overview
- More than 40% of all motor vehicle fatalities in NY involve impaired driving
- Drivers under 21 represent 4% of the driving population but 7% of impaired drivers in fatal crashes

## Blood Alcohol Content (BAC) Basics
- BAC = percentage of alcohol in your blood; determined by chemical test of breath, blood, urine, or saliva
- Factors affecting BAC: amount of alcohol consumed, time between drinks, body weight
- BAC does NOT depend on: type of beverage, physical fitness, individual tolerance
- Women process alcohol more slowly than men
- Body takes approximately one hour to remove one standard drink's worth of alcohol

**Standard Drink Equivalents:**
- 1.5 oz liquor (80 proof)
- 5 oz wine (12% alcohol)
- 12 oz beer (5% alcohol)
- 12 oz wine cooler (5% alcohol)

## Alcohol's Effects on Driving
- Delays reaction time
- Reduces vision clarity
- Distorts judgment of speed and distances
- Reduces inhibition, increases risk-taking
- Impairs decision-making
- Affects nighttime driving particularly dangerously (reduces recovery from headlight glare)
- Effects begin "after just one drink" at BAC levels lower than legal limits

## Drugs and Driving
- Many prescription and over-the-counter medications can affect driving ability
- Combining alcohol with other drugs could be dangerous, often enhancing effects of both
- Example: one drink while using cold remedies may affect driving equivalent to several drinks
- Covered substances: marijuana/cannabis, cocaine, LSD, heroin, opium, certain prescription medications

## Offense Classification & BAC Thresholds

| Offense | BAC | Description |
|---------|-----|-------------|
| Aggravated DWI (Agg-DWI) | 0.18% or higher | Evidence of aggravated driving while intoxicated |
| DWI (Driving While Intoxicated) | 0.08% or higher | Evidence of intoxication |
| DWAI (Driving While Ability Impaired — alcohol) | 0.05%–0.07% | More than .05% is legal evidence of impairment |
| DWAI-Drug | Any amount | Impairment demonstrated by drugs |
| DWAI-Combination | Any | Impairment from both alcohol and drugs |
| Zero Tolerance (Under 21) | 0.02%–0.07% | Any measurable BAC |

## Proof of Intoxication
- Testimony of a police officer about the way you drive and your appearance/behavior can provide enough evidence to convict you even without a chemical test

## Implied Consent Law & Chemical Tests
- When you drive in NY, you are considered to have already given your consent to a chemical test
- **Refusal consequences:**
  - License suspension upon arraignment
  - Refusal admissible as evidence in trial
  - If DMV hearing confirms refusal, license revocation occurs even if found not guilty of the alcohol/drug violation

## Conviction Penalties Summary

### Aggravated DWI (0.18+ BAC)
**1st Offense (Misdemeanor):**
- Fine: $1,000–$2,500; Jail: up to 1 year; License: minimum 1-year revocation

**2nd Offense within 10 years (Class E Felony):**
- Fine: $1,000–$5,000; Jail: up to 4 years; License: minimum 18-month revocation

### DWI (0.08+ BAC) or DWAI-Drug
**1st Offense (Misdemeanor):**
- Fine: $500–$1,000; Jail: up to 1 year; License: minimum 6-month revocation

**2nd Offense within 10 years (Class E Felony):**
- Fine: $1,000–$5,000; Jail: up to 4 years (minimum 5 days jail or 30 days community service)
- License: minimum 1-year revocation

### DWAI (0.05–0.07 BAC)
**1st Offense (Traffic Infraction):**
- Fine: $300–$500; Jail: up to 15 days; License: 90-day suspension

**2nd Offense within 5 years (Traffic Infraction):**
- Fine: $500–$750; Jail: up to 30 days; License: minimum 6-month revocation

**3rd Offense within 10 years (Misdemeanor):**
- Fine: $750–$1,500; Jail: up to 180 days; License: minimum 6-month revocation

### Zero Tolerance (Under Age 21)
**1st Violation:**
- License suspension: 6 months
- Fees: $100 suspension termination fee + $125 civil penalty for re-licensing

**2nd Violation:**
- License revocation: minimum 1 year or until age 21, whichever is longer

### Open Container Law
- Applies to drivers or passengers drinking or possessing open alcoholic containers on public roads
- Also applies to consuming marijuana/cannabis
- Fine: up to $150 (first conviction); jail: up to 15 days possible
- Exemption: passengers in stretch limousines and vehicles with DOT/NYS DOT commerce certificate

## Chemical Test Refusal Penalties
- 21+: minimum 1-year license revocation + $500 civil penalty
- 21+, within 5 years of prior: minimum 18-month revocation + $750 civil penalty
- Under 21, first: minimum 1-year revocation
- Under 21, second: minimum 1-year or until age 21 (whichever longer)

## Repeat Offender Policies

### Permanent License Denial
Denial if applicant has:
- Four alcohol/drug-related driving convictions or incidents in lifetime, OR
- Three alcohol/drug-related convictions within 25-year period plus one serious driving offense

### Delayed Re-Licensing
For drivers with three alcohol/drug-related convictions within 25 years (no serious driving offense):
- Additional 5-year wait beyond statutory revocation if revoked for alcohol/drug offense
- Additional 2-year wait if revoked for other reason
- After additional period: license restored as "restricted" for work, medical visits, essential destinations
- Must use ignition interlock device for 5 years from date of issuance

## Ignition Interlock Program & Leandra's Law
- Courts must order ignition interlock installation for all convicted of DWI, Agg-DWI, or penal law offenses
- Duration: minimum 12 months (may be waived if installed for at least 6 months, unless court orders longer)
- Cost: purchased and installed at expense of the motorist
- Device connects to ignition; vehicle cannot start until driver provides acceptable breath sample

### Leandra's Law
- Class E felony (up to 4 years prison) when alcohol-related offense committed with child under 16 in vehicle

## Driver Responsibility Assessment
- For Agg-DWI, DWI, DWAI, DWAI-drug, or chemical test refusal: $250 per year for 3 years
- For 6+ points in 18-month period: $100 per year for 3 years + $25 per additional point per year

## Additional Consequences
- Liability insurance may not cover crash costs
- Civil lawsuits possible for thousands of dollars
- Difficulty obtaining insurance for years; costly legal fees
- Criminal record affects employment prospects
- Resulting in death or injury: convictions for criminally negligent homicide, vehicular manslaughter carry maximum jail up to 25 years

## Commercial Driver Standards
- BAC standards and penalties for commercial drivers are stricter than those in this chapter
