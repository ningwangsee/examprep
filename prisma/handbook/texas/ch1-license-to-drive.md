# Chapter 1: Your License to Drive
Source: https://www.dps.texas.gov/internetforms/Forms/DL-7.pdf#page=8
(Texas Driver Handbook DL-7, Jan 2026)

## Age Requirements

- **Learner License (Hardship):** Age 15 with hardship circumstances
- **Learner License (standard):** Age 15 — may drive with a licensed adult 21+ in the front seat
- **Provisional License (Phase I):** Age 16 — issued after passing knowledge and driving skills tests; valid 1 year
- **Full License (Phase II / Unrestricted):** Age 18 — issued after holding provisional license at least 12 months
- Minors under 18 must complete an approved driver education course

## Texas Graduated Driver License (GDL) Program

### Learner License (under 18)
- Must have a licensed adult 21 or older in the front seat at all times
- No driving between midnight and 5 a.m. (unless work/school related with a note)
- Must hold learner license at least 6 months before applying for provisional

### Provisional License (Phase I, age 16–17)
- No driving between midnight and 5 a.m. (exceptions: medical emergency, work, school activity with note)
- For first 6 months: may not drive with more than 1 passenger under 21 (unless family member)
- Must complete driver education course (approved DE program)
- No use of handheld wireless communication devices while driving

### Full License (age 18+)
- All adult driving privileges
- Same rules apply as adults regarding BAC limits and traffic laws

## Driver Education
- Minors under 18 must complete a Texas Education Agency (TEA)-approved driver education course
- Course includes 32 hours of classroom instruction (or online equivalent) + 7 hours behind the wheel

## Required Documents to Apply
- Proof of identity (birth certificate, passport, etc.)
- Social Security number
- Texas residency (two documents)
- Parental/guardian consent (if under 18)
- Certificate of completion of driver education course
- Vision screening

## Vision Standards
- Minimum visual acuity: 20/40 in at least one eye (with or without corrective lenses)
- Visual field: minimum 140 degrees in the horizontal plane

## Knowledge and Skills Tests
- Knowledge test: multiple-choice, based on the Texas Driver Handbook
- Must score at least 70% to pass
- Road skills test: evaluated on parking, turns, lane changes, intersections, etc.

## License Renewal
- Texas driver licenses are valid for up to 8 years (age 18–84)
- Drivers 85+ must renew every 2 years
- Military personnel stationed out of state may obtain a temporary extension

## License Classes
- **Class A:** Commercial vehicles with GVWR 26,001+ lbs towing 10,001+ lbs
- **Class B:** Commercial vehicles with GVWR 26,001+ lbs
- **Class C:** Non-commercial vehicles, most passenger cars
- **Class M:** Motorcycles

## Financial Responsibility (Insurance)
- Texas requires minimum liability insurance:
  - $30,000 bodily injury per person
  - $60,000 bodily injury per accident
  - $25,000 property damage per accident
- Must carry proof of insurance while driving
- Failure to maintain insurance can result in license suspension and fines

## Accident Reporting
- Must report any accident involving injury, death, or property damage over $1,000 to the Texas Department of Transportation
- Driver's license can be suspended if you fail to report or maintain insurance

## Points and Surcharges (Driver Responsibility Program)
- Texas uses a Driver Responsibility Program (DRP) with surcharges for certain convictions
- 6+ points in 36 months: annual surcharge of $100 plus $25 per additional point
- DWI conviction: $1,000–$2,000 annual surcharge for 3 years
- No valid insurance: $250/year surcharge for 3 years
- Note: As of Sept 2019, DRP was repealed; however, surcharge history may still appear for prior convictions
