# Chapter 14: Safety Tips (Pedestrians, Bicycles, Crashes, Sharing the Road)
Source: https://www.dps.texas.gov/internetforms/Forms/DL-7.pdf#page=74
(Texas Driver Handbook DL-7, Jan 2026)

## Seat Belts

### Texas Seat Belt Law
- All occupants in the front seat must wear a seat belt
- All occupants in the back seat must wear a seat belt
- **Age 8 and under** (or under 4'9" tall): must be in a child passenger safety seat system
- Driver is responsible for passengers under age 18
- Fine for adult not wearing seat belt: up to **$200**
- Fine for child passenger not in proper restraint: up to **$250**

### Why Seat Belts Save Lives
- Seat belts reduce the risk of death in a crash by about 45% for front-seat occupants
- Reduce the risk of serious injury by 50%
- Prevent being thrown from the vehicle
- Airbags work with seat belts — alone, airbags do not provide full protection

## Child Passenger Safety

### Car Seat Requirements (Texas Transportation Code §545.412)
- **Under age 8 AND under 4'9":** must use a child passenger safety seat system
- **Rear-facing infant seat:** recommended for newborns until they exceed the seat's weight/height limit (typically age 2+)
- **Forward-facing with harness:** after outgrowing rear-facing; typically until age 4–5 or 40–65 lbs
- **Booster seat:** after outgrowing harness seat; until the seat belt fits properly (usually 8–12 years or 4'9"+)
- **Seat belt:** when child is 8+ or at least 4'9" tall
- Car seat must be installed correctly; follow manufacturer's instructions

## Crash Procedures

### What to Do After a Crash
1. **Stop immediately** — leaving the scene is a serious crime (hit and run)
2. **Move to safety** if possible — move vehicles out of traffic to prevent further accidents
3. **Call 911** — if there are injuries, deaths, or significant property damage
4. **Provide assistance** — render first aid if trained; do not move seriously injured persons unless there is immediate danger (fire, etc.)
5. **Exchange information:**
   - Name, address, phone number
   - Driver's license number
   - Vehicle registration/license plate
   - Insurance company and policy number
6. **Report to authorities** — file a written report with TxDOT if damage exceeds $1,000 or there are injuries/deaths
7. **Do not admit fault** at the scene

### When You Must Report a Crash
- Any crash involving injury or death
- Property damage to any vehicle/property over **$1,000**
- Report within 10 days to TxDOT

### Hit and Run (Leaving the Scene)
- Failure to stop and render aid when injury or death is involved: **second-degree felony**
- Failure to stop for property damage only: **Class C misdemeanor**

## Sharing the Road

### With Pedestrians
- Yield to pedestrians in all crosswalks (marked and unmarked)
- Yield to pedestrians on sidewalks when crossing a driveway
- Always yield to blind persons with white cane or guide dog
- Be especially alert near schools, parks, playgrounds, and bus stops
- Never park on a sidewalk or in a crosswalk
- Pedestrians have right-of-way at all intersections when crossing with the signal

### With Bicyclists
- Bicycles have the same rights and duties as motor vehicles on Texas roads
- Pass a bicycle with at least **3 feet of clearance** (Texas Transportation Code §545.053)
- Yield to cyclists at intersections and crosswalks
- Check for cyclists before opening your car door (dooring)
- Bicycle lanes: do not park or drive in bicycle lanes except to cross them

### With School Buses
- When a school bus stops and displays flashing red lights AND extends the stop arm:
  - **All traffic in both directions on an undivided road MUST STOP** and remain stopped
  - On a **divided highway** (with a raised median or barrier): only traffic behind the bus must stop; oncoming traffic may proceed
- You must remain stopped until all children have boarded or exited and the stop arm is retracted
- Penalty for passing an active school bus: Class A misdemeanor; fine up to $1,250 for first offense; $2,000 for subsequent offenses

### With Large Trucks
- Trucks have large blind spots (No-Zones): in front, behind, and on both sides
- Stay out of a truck's No-Zones — if you cannot see the driver in their mirror, they cannot see you
- Do not cut off trucks — they need much more stopping distance
- Pass trucks quickly and completely
- Allow extra space when following a truck (debris hazard)

## Railroad Crossings

### At Railroad Crossings
- **Crossbuck sign (X):** yield; treat as a YIELD sign if no other signals
- **Flashing lights + bells:** stop; train is approaching
- **Gates lowered:** stop; must not proceed until gates fully rise
- Stop at least **15 feet** from the nearest rail
- Never stop on the railroad tracks; wait for sufficient space on the other side
- If stalled on tracks: get everyone out immediately and move away from the tracks at a 45° angle away from the direction the train is coming from

### Look Both Ways
- Even if one train passes, another may come from the opposite direction immediately
- Never race a train to a crossing

## Drowsy Driving

### Warning Signs of Drowsiness
- Difficulty keeping eyes open or focused
- Trouble keeping head up
- Drifting from your lane
- Missing exits or traffic signs
- Cannot remember the last few miles driven

### Prevention
- Get 7–9 hours of sleep before a long trip
- Stop and rest every 2 hours or 100 miles
- Take a 15–20 minute nap in a safe location if needed
- Caffeine provides temporary relief but is not a substitute for sleep
- Avoid driving between midnight and 6 a.m. (peak drowsiness window)
- Do not rely on rumble strips — they are a last resort, not a safety plan

## Vehicle Control and Emergency Maneuvers

### Tire Blowout
- Keep a firm grip on the steering wheel
- Ease off the accelerator
- Do not brake suddenly — let the vehicle slow down gradually
- Steer to maintain control; pull off the road when safe

### Brake Failure
- Pump the brakes (may restore hydraulic pressure)
- Downshift to lower gear
- Use the parking/emergency brake gradually
- Steer toward a safe area to slow the vehicle (uphill, grass, gravel)

### Skids
- **Rear-wheel skid (oversteer):** steer in the direction the rear is sliding; ease off the gas
- **Front-wheel skid (understeer):** take foot off gas; let vehicle slow; steer gently when traction returns
- With ABS: apply firm, steady pressure — do not pump; the system pulses for you
