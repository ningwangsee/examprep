# Chapter 5: Signals, Signs, and Markings
Source: https://www.dps.texas.gov/internetforms/Forms/DL-7.pdf#page=34
(Texas Driver Handbook DL-7, Jan 2026)

## Traffic Signals

### Traffic Lights
- **Green:** Go — proceed through intersection if clear; yield to pedestrians and cross traffic still in intersection
- **Yellow:** Warning — signal is changing to red; stop if you can do so safely; if too close to stop, proceed with caution
- **Red:** Stop — stop before the intersection/crosswalk/stop line and remain stopped
- **Red + right turn arrow:** Stop; may turn right after stopping if no conflicting traffic/pedestrians (unless posted NO TURN ON RED)
- **Flashing Red:** Treat as a STOP sign — stop completely, then proceed when safe
- **Flashing Yellow:** Treat as a YIELD sign — slow down, proceed with caution
- **Green Arrow:** May proceed in the direction of the arrow; oncoming traffic has a red signal

### Lane Control Signals (overhead)
- **Green Arrow (pointing down):** May use this lane
- **Red X:** Do NOT use this lane; illegal to travel in this lane
- **Yellow X (flashing):** Lane is transitioning; prepare to change out of this lane

### Pedestrian Signals
- **WALK / white walking figure:** Pedestrians may cross
- **DON'T WALK / orange hand:** Pedestrians should not start crossing
- **Flashing DON'T WALK / flashing hand + countdown:** Pedestrians already crossing may finish; do not start a new crossing

## Traffic Signs: Shape and Color Code

### By Shape
| Shape | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| Octagon (8 sides) | STOP only |
| Equilateral triangle (point down) | YIELD only |
| Diamond | Warning |
| Rectangle (vertical) | Regulatory |
| Rectangle (horizontal) | Guide/informational |
| Pentagon (5 sides, point up) | School zone |
| Round | Railroad crossing advance warning |
| Crossbuck (X) | Railroad crossing |
| Pennant (pointed right) | No Passing Zone |

### By Color
| Color | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| Red | Stop, yield, prohibition |
| Yellow | Warning, caution |
| Orange | Construction/maintenance work zone warning |
| Green | Guide, permitted movements, distance |
| Blue | Services (gas, food, lodging, hospital) |
| Brown | Recreation, cultural, scenic |
| White | Regulatory (speed limits, lane controls) |
| Black | Supplementary info on white signs |
| Fluorescent Yellow-Green | School, pedestrian, and bicycle areas |

## Regulatory Signs
- **STOP (red octagon):** Come to a complete stop; proceed when safe
- **YIELD (red triangle):** Slow down; give right-of-way; stop if necessary
- **Speed Limit:** Maximum speed under ideal conditions; must post white rectangular sign
- **No Left Turn / No Right Turn / No U-Turn:** Prohibited turns
- **Do Not Enter / Wrong Way:** Do not proceed in that direction
- **One Way:** Traffic flows only in the direction shown
- **Keep Right:** Stay to the right of a divider or island
- **No Passing Zone (pennant):** Passing prohibited; appears on left side of road

## Warning Signs (Yellow Diamond)
- Curve ahead, sharp turn, hill, dip, bump
- Slippery when wet
- Deer crossing, cattle crossing, pedestrian crossing
- School crossing (fluorescent yellow-green pentagon)
- Intersection ahead
- Railroad crossing (round, yellow, with X and RR)
- Bridge/underpass ahead

## Construction Zone Signs (Orange)
- All standard construction warning signs are orange diamond shapes
- Fines doubled in active work zones when workers are present
- Workers ahead, lane closed, road construction, flaggers

## Guide Signs
- **Interstate signs (blue/red shield):** Identify interstate highways; even numbers = east-west, odd numbers = north-south
- **US Route (white shield):** Federal highways
- **Texas State (white outline of Texas):** State highways
- **Farm-to-Market (FM) / Ranch-to-Market (RM):** State-maintained rural roads in Texas; unique to Texas
- **Green rectangular signs:** Highway directional signs, city names, distances, mile markers, exit numbers

## Pavement Markings

### Yellow Lines (separate opposing traffic)
- **Solid yellow (single):** No passing from either direction
- **Double solid yellow:** No passing from either direction; may not cross
- **Solid yellow on your side + dashed on other side:** You may not pass; oncoming may pass
- **Dashed yellow:** Passing permitted when safe from either side

### White Lines (same-direction traffic or edge)
- **Solid white (single):** Edge of travel lane or stop line; do not cross unless entering/exiting
- **Dashed white:** Lane division within same-direction flow; may change lanes when safe
- **Double solid white:** No lane changes permitted (HOV entry/exit, intersection)
- **Stop line (solid wide white):** Stop behind this line at signal or sign
- **Crosswalk lines:** Mark pedestrian crossing area
- **Arrows and words:** Lane direction and restrictions painted on road surface

### Special Markings
- **Raised pavement markers (RPMs / Botts' dots):** Aid in lane tracking at night or in rain
- **Railroad crossing (RXR):** Painted on road surface ahead of crossing; sometimes with "STOP AHEAD"
- **Bicycle lane markings:** White pavement markings + bicycle symbol
- **HOV/Carpool markings:** Diamond symbol in HOV lanes
