# Chapter 4: Traffic Laws (Right-of-Way, Turns, Passing, Parking)
Source: https://www.dps.texas.gov/internetforms/Forms/DL-7.pdf#page=26
(Texas Driver Handbook DL-7, Jan 2026)

## Right-of-Way Rules

### At Uncontrolled Intersections
- The vehicle that arrives first has the right-of-way
- If two vehicles arrive simultaneously, the driver on the LEFT yields to the driver on the RIGHT
- Never assume another driver will yield — proceed only when it is safe

### At Four-Way Stops
- Vehicle that arrives first goes first
- If simultaneous arrival: driver on left yields to driver on right
- Straight-through traffic has priority over turning traffic when facing each other
- Always yield to pedestrians

### Yielding to Emergency Vehicles
- When any emergency vehicle approaches with lights or siren, immediately pull to the right and stop
- If in an intersection, clear the intersection first, then pull over
- Remain stopped until the emergency vehicle passes
- Applies on all roads, both directions

### Move Over Law (Texas Transportation Code §545.157)
- On a multi-lane road: vacate the lane closest to a stationary authorized emergency vehicle, tow truck, or TxDOT vehicle with lights on
- If unable to move over safely: reduce speed to at least 20 mph below the posted speed limit
- On roads with a speed limit of 25 mph or less: reduce speed to 5 mph
- Failure to comply: Class B misdemeanor (up to $200 fine; if property damage involved, up to $500; if injury, Class A misdemeanor)

### Pedestrian Right-of-Way
- Yield to pedestrians in both marked and unmarked crosswalks at intersections
- Never pass a vehicle stopped for a pedestrian in a crosswalk
- Always yield to a person using a white cane or guide dog (visually impaired) — even if not in a crosswalk
- Pedestrian has right-of-way at any intersection or crosswalk

### Bicycle Right-of-Way
- Bicycles have the same rights and duties as motor vehicles on Texas roads
- Pass a bicycle with at least 3 feet of clearance
- Yield to bicycles as you would to any other vehicle

## Turning

### Right Turns
- Signal at least 100 feet before turning
- Stay close to the right curb or edge before, during, and after the turn
- Turn into the nearest right lane of the road you are entering

### Left Turns
- Signal at least 100 feet before turning
- Approach from the leftmost lane (or turn lane)
- Yield to all oncoming vehicles and pedestrians before turning
- Turn into the leftmost available lane on the road you are entering

### U-Turns
- Permitted at most Texas intersections unless posted otherwise
- Prohibited on curves or hills where you cannot be seen at least 500 feet in each direction
- Prohibited in school zones
- Prohibited where a sign prohibits U-turns
- Prohibited where it would interfere with traffic

### Signaling Requirements
- Signal at least 100 feet before turning or changing lanes in a city/residential area
- On highways/higher-speed roads, signal sufficiently in advance
- Continuous signal required — do not signal after you begin the turn

## Lane Usage and Passing

### Lane Changes
- Signal, check mirrors, check blind spots (look over your shoulder), then move
- Never force other drivers to brake or swerve
- Complete the lane change safely before reaching any intersection

### Passing (Overtaking)
- Pass on the left when the center line is a dashed yellow line and the road ahead is clear
- You may pass on the right when: (1) the vehicle ahead is turning left, or (2) there are two or more lanes of traffic moving the same direction
- Never pass on the right by driving on the shoulder or unpaved area
- Do not pass within 100 feet of an intersection, railroad crossing, bridge, or tunnel
- Do not pass on a curve or hill where visibility is limited
- Do not pass where a solid yellow line is on your side of the center

### No-Passing Zones
- Indicated by a solid yellow line on your side, or a NO PASSING ZONE pennant sign
- Do not enter the oncoming lane in these areas

## Parking

### Where Parking Is Prohibited
- Within **15 feet** of a fire hydrant
- In an intersection
- On a crosswalk
- Within **20 feet** of a crosswalk at an intersection
- Within **30 feet** of a traffic signal, stop sign, or yield sign
- On a sidewalk
- Blocking a driveway, alley entrance, or fire station driveway
- On a bridge or overpass
- In a tunnel
- In a space for persons with disabilities (unless you have a valid placard/license plate)
- Within **50 feet** of a railroad crossing

### Parking on Hills
- **Facing uphill WITH curb:** Turn wheels AWAY from curb (to the left); if brakes fail, wheel catches on curb
- **Facing downhill WITH curb:** Turn wheels TOWARD curb (to the right); vehicle rolls into curb, not traffic
- **No curb (either direction):** Turn wheels to the RIGHT so vehicle rolls off road rather than into traffic
- Set parking brake; leave vehicle in park (automatic) or in gear (manual)

### Distance from Curb
- Park within **18 inches** of the curb in a parallel space

## Traffic Signals Behavior

### Red Light
- Stop before the stop line, crosswalk, or intersection
- Right turn on red permitted after full stop unless posted otherwise (NO TURN ON RED sign)
- Left turn on red from a one-way street onto another one-way street: permitted after full stop

### Yellow Light
- Warning: light is about to turn red
- Stop if you can do so safely
- If too close to stop safely, proceed through with caution
- Never accelerate to "beat" a yellow light

### Green Light
- Proceed if intersection is clear; yield to pedestrians and traffic already in the intersection

### Flashing Signals
- Flashing red = STOP sign (stop completely, proceed when safe)
- Flashing yellow = YIELD (slow, proceed with caution)
